The Game

Built from scratch, shipped for real.

Commando Bob is a mobile action game built solo in Unity with C#. Every system was written from scratch — character movement, enemy AI, level design, UI, animations, and sound integration. No game jams, no tutorials followed to completion. Just building until it worked.

Getting it published on the Google Play Store required learning not just game development but app signing, Play Console submission, store listing optimization, and update management. It was a full product launch — shipped by one person.

🕹️
Core Gameplay
Mobile action mechanics with touch controls — designed specifically for phone play. Character movement, combat, and progression systems all built in Unity's MonoBehaviour architecture.
🤖
Enemy AI
Custom enemy behavior implemented in C# — patrol patterns, detection ranges, attack logic, and state machines. Each enemy type had distinct behavior to create varied encounters.
🎨
Level Design
Multiple levels designed and built using Unity's scene system — balancing difficulty, pacing, and replayability. Everything from layout to enemy placement to environmental hazards.
📱
Published & Live
Went through the full Google Play publishing pipeline — app signing, store listing, content rating, release management. A real product, not a prototype that lived on a hard drive.

Why It Matters

The project that started everything.

Commando Bob was the project where I learned what it actually meant to finish something. Not finish as in "it runs on my machine" — finish as in publish, ship, and let strangers use it. That experience of closing the loop — from idea to real product in people's hands — became the standard I hold every project to.

It's also why I gravitate toward building over theorizing. The game is gone from the Play Store, but the habit it built stuck around.
